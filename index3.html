Bangladesh is celebrating the Golden Jubilee in 2021. The country is also observing the Birth Centenary of the Father of the Nation Bangabandhu Sheikh Mujibur Rahman. 2021 also marks 50 years of Bangladesh-UK diplomatic relations. To celebrate these historic occasions, Bangladesh High Commission, London has taken the initiative to take forward digital economy partnership with the UK to a whole new level enhancing digital trade and digital investment between Bangladesh and UK. In collaboration with the ICT Division of the government of Bangladesh, through the Bangladesh IT Connect, Bangladesh High Commission, London seeks to unfold to the UK’s IT businesses a Digital Bangladesh pioneered by our visionary Prime Minister H.E. Sheikh Hasina and steered by our ICT Adviser and knowledge leader H.E Sajeeb Wazed Joy. An indomitable Bangladesh that is on the move, an energetic nation and a vibrant digital economy in South Asia is ready for 4IR with a vibrant digital economy. Through this portal, UK government, businesses and investors will be able to find their right technology and BPO partner in Bangladesh. They will also be able to register for and participate in the B2B events organized by Bangladesh High Commission, London. United Kingdom is currently not only Bangladesh’s 3rd largest trading partner but also the 2nd largest IT and ITeS export destination. Leading UK companies are placing their trust in Bangladesh for procuring Business Process Outsourcing services, back-office support, software development, web application development, blockchain, Artificial Intelligence, Internet of Things, Graphic Design, Virtual Reality, Robotics and many more technology-based solutions and services. Today, Bangladesh is known as a development miracle. It is recognized as the role model for socio-economic development in the world. Even during the COVID-19 Pandemic, Bangladesh’s economy has grown at 5.24%. Today’s Bangladesh is not only world’s 8th largest digital market and South Asia’s growth leader, but also a prime supplier of IT products and services to more than 35 countries including the United Kingdom. We are also proud of our 1 million plus digital workforce who are catering to the global digital business and industry. Bangladesh also boasts of an innovative generation of young entrepreneurs who are hungry for developing the world’s most influential start-ups and tech enterprises leveraging start-up funds and infrastructure provided by the government in our 39 Hi-tech parks across Bangladesh. There are limitless opportunities for British IT companies to invest in Bangladeshi start-ups, fintech and creative digital industry under a liberal public incentives regime with 100% profit repatriation. I firmly belief that the existing bilateral relations between Bangladesh and UK would further grow through this portal.

I would like to thank Bangladesh High Commission, London for their active role in setting up the Bangladesh IT Connect platform. Our Father of the Nation Bangabandhu Sheikh Mujibur Rahman is often called a friend of the World, for his fight for the rights of deprived people and his sacrifice for shaping war-torn Bangladesh as a nation. He travelled across the world envisioned to create friends of Bangladesh. Bangabandhu visited London in 1972 to meet the then UK Prime Minister Edward Heath. After a month of their meeting, Britain recognised Bangladesh as an independent country in 1972. This led to the eventual recognition of Bangladesh by other European and Commonwealth nations and Bangladesh's induction into the Commonwealth. The bond he had established with the UK continues to guide the bilateral relations between Bangladesh and the UK in the post-Brexit and post-Covid era. Under the leadership of our Prime Minister Sheikh Hasina, Bangladesh has been able to manage the pandemic with fewer deaths and contamination and our economy is regaining its loss faster than most countries. Bangladesh was able to achieve positive growth during the last 8 months of the pandemic. Bangladesh is one of only two ASEAN and South Asian economies, to register positive growth in 2020 despite a global recession. We have used the home-grown Digital Technologies to sustain our economy – from working from home to maintaining the agriculture supply chain, Digital has played a key role in achieving the 5.2 percent GDP growth till June 2020. Local technology companies have played an integral role in our crisis response. Digital tools are being used efficiently by the government to monitor, track and to contain the spread of the virus, map hotspots, keep people informed, and also to deliver welfare measures and citizen services. Digital services like telemedicine, live update, self-testing tool, helplines, AI-powered chat-bot, contract tracer, mobile apps, mobile financial services, digital commerce, digital content and videos have been making the lives of the citizens easier during the pandemic. This journey started 11 years ago, with the declaration of the Digital Bangladesh Vision by the Honorable Prime Minister Sheikh Hasina. She has envisioned 3 major milestones for Bangladesh – Vision 2021 to achieve Digital Bangladesh by reaching middle-income status, Vision 2030 to achieve SDG’s and to take our GDP from 348 Billion US Dollars today, to 700 Billion US dollars by 2030; which would make Bangladesh the 26th largest economy, and Vision 2041 to achieve knowledge economy and high-income country with per capita income of over 12,000 US dollars by 2041. UK is the second-largest market in terms of export revenue for the Bangladeshi Technology and BPO industry. For nearly a decade, Bangladeshi technology and BPO companies have been working in the UK Market with success. The Bangladesh IT Connect will foster partnership and collaboration with the UK through investment, trade and business match-making, further contribute to our IT export target of USD 5 billion by year 2025.
